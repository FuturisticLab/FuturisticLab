Welcome to the Nexus thermodynamic simulation dashboard. This console plots the physical behavior of a complete 4-stage vapor compression cooling cycle.

💡 Concept: The cycle shifts in real-time as you modify parameters. Click on any component box (Evaporator, Compressor, etc.) to load direct stage analysis.

Stage 1: The Evaporator (Indoor Coil)

A cold, low-pressure liquid-vapor refrigerant mixture flows inside. Warm air from the house is blown over the coils, forcing the refrigerant to absorb heat and boil into a gas.

Phase Transformation Liquid ➔ Vapor
Pressure State LOW (118 PSI)

Superheat: Saturated boiling completes inside, and the vapor warms slightly as it exits, ensuring no liquid reaches the compressor.

Stage 2: The Compressor

The "heart" of the system. Cool gas is sucked in and compressed into a high-pressure, superheated hot gas. Squeezing the molecules creates friction and kinetic energy, driving the temperature up.

Phase Transformation Vapor ➔ Hot Vapor
Pressure State Low ➔ High

Power: This stage accounts for approximately 90% of the electrical energy consumed by the cooling system.

Stage 3: The Condenser (Outdoor Coil)

The high-pressure, superheated vapor enters the outdoor coil. Because the refrigerant is hotter than the outside air, heat flows out of the system. As it cools, the gas condenses back to a warm liquid.

Phase Transformation Vapor ➔ Liquid
Pressure State HIGH (418 PSI)

Subcooling: The liquid refrigerant cools below its condensing temperature before leaving, maximizing downstream expansion capacity.

Stage 4: Expansion Valve

The warm liquid is forced through a microscopic orifice. The drop in pressure causes flash evaporation, forcing the refrigerant temperature to plummet down to its boiling point.

Phase Transformation Liquid ➔ Mix
Pressure State High ➔ Low

Throttling: An isenthalpic process (constant enthalpy). The pressure relief creates a fine, cold mist, primed to absorb heat.

🔍 Heat Absorption vs Heat Rejection

🏠 Indoor Unit (Evaporator)

  • Sensible Action: Sucks in hot indoor air, blows out cooled indoor air.
  • Refrigerant State: Low pressure cold liquid boils to a gas.
  • Heat Direction: Heat energy flows from the room air INTO the refrigerant (Endothermic).
  • Coil Temperature: Kept at ~40°F (4°C) to facilitate massive heat exchange.
  • Comfort Effect: Removes humidity as water condenses on the cold coil surfaces.

🌞 Outdoor Unit (Condenser)

  • Sensible Action: Sucks in ambient outside air, blows out high-heat air.
  • Refrigerant State: High pressure hot vapor cools and condenses to a liquid.
  • Heat Direction: Heat energy flows from the refrigerant OUT to the environment (Exothermic).
  • Coil Temperature: Elevates to ~120°F (49°C) to dump heat into hot summer air.
  • Power Action: Contains the compressor motor pulling current to drive the thermodynamic work.
